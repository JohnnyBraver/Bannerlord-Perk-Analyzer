# Low-Investment Perk Pickups

With 2 attribute and 1 focus, a skill has a limit of 58 and a peak learning range of 40. That is enough for the level 25 and level 50 perk choices, so a single focus point can unlock the first two perk tiers in any skill whose attribute is at least 2.

This is one of the best places to look for quality-of-life picks. Character creation focus counts, so a background choice can sometimes cover the whole splash by itself.

## Best Quality-of-Life Splashes

### Trade

One focus point into Trade is one of the best quality-of-life upgrades in the game.

At level 25, both `Appraiser` and `Whole Seller` mark profitable trades in the UI. The difference is the economic side bonus: `Appraiser` reduces the selling penalty for equipment, while `Whole Seller` reduces the selling penalty for trade goods.

At level 50, both `Caravan Master` and `Market Dealer` mark item prices relative to average price. `Caravan Master` is usually the better practical pick because it also gives the quartermaster +30% party carrying capacity. `Market Dealer` is more niche unless safe-passage barter costs matter to the run.

Takeaway: even if the build is not a trade build, getting Trade to 50 makes shopping, loot selling, and trade-good decisions much less noisy.

### Medicine

Medicine has a similar quality-of-life effect because the early perks reduce downtime after fights.

At level 25, `Preventive Medicine` gives the hero +5 hit points and recovers 30% of lost hit points after each battle. This is a personal perk, so the post-battle recovery belongs to the hero who took it.

At level 50, `Walk It Off` gives the surgeon +15% healing rate while moving on the campaign map and gives the hero 10 hit points after each offensive battle. `Triage Tent` is better when the party is stationary, but `Walk It Off` is usually the smoother roaming pick.

Takeaway: a small Medicine splash on the player character is very noticeable in normal campaign play, especially when chaining fights.

## Other Strong Early Pickups

| Skill | Best low-investment reason | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Steward | Food, wages, and passive troop XP. | `Warrior's Diet` removes the morale penalty from single-food parties and reduces food consumption. `Frugal` saves wages/recruitment cost. At 50, `Drill Sergeant` gives daily XP to all troops, while `Seven Veterans` targets tier 4+ troops. Best if the character is quartermaster. |
| Scouting | Map speed, sight range, and relation trickles. | `Day Traveler` and `Night Runner` are scout-role campaign-map comfort perks. At 50, `Pathfinder` or `Water Diviner` can add notable-relation chances when entering towns or villages. |
| Leadership | Early troop XP and prisoner conversion. | `Combat Tips` and `Raise The Meek` are useful early training perks. At 50, the choice is mostly attack/defense morale plus which prisoner tiers recruit faster. |
| Athletics | Personal durability and foot-combat comfort. | `Well Built` is a clean +5 personal HP and +5 HP to foot troops in the party. At 50, `Form Fitting Armor` is a strong armor-weight quality-of-life perk, while `Fury` is better for weapon handling. |
| Riding | Mounted comfort and mount protection. | `Nimble Steed` improves riding and gives a captain skill bonus; `Full Speed` is charge damage. At 50, `Well Strapped` protects your mount from death or lameness, while `Veterinary` adds mount HP. |
| Smithing | Early crafting economy and unlock speed. | `Efficient Charcoal Maker` is excellent because charcoal production becomes both more efficient and a surprisingly good early Smithing XP loop. At 50, `Curious Smelter` doubles part-learning from smelting. |
| Charm | Early renown, influence, and relationship utility. | `Self Promoter` helps tournament-renown starts. `Warlord` gives +30% influence from battles, while `Oratory` rewards issue-solving. Useful if the run is already leaning into social play. |
| Roguery | Tournament betting, bandits, and sneaking. | `Deep Pockets` doubles tournament betting. `Two Faced` helps sneaking into towns and removes morale loss from converting bandit prisoners. Niche, but handy for that playstyle. |
| Engineering | Siege specialist utility. | The first two tiers are good if the character is your engineer: ranged/non-ranged siege build speed, siege engine durability, and a governor prebuilt-catapult perk. Low priority outside siege-focused roles. |
| Tactics | Auto-resolve and terrain-captain play. | The first personal effects are for troops sent to confront the enemy, so they matter mainly if you use simulation/auto-resolve. The captain effects are formation morale or terrain movement bonuses. |

## Mostly Build-Dependent

The early weapon-skill perks are usually good only when the character personally uses that weapon or captains the matching formation.

| Skill | Early value |
| --- | --- |
| One Handed | `Wrapped Handles` is a strong handling/infantry-skill pick; `Basher` is shield-bash and shield-wall mitigation. |
| Two Handed | `Strong Grip` and `Wood Chopper` are solid for two-handed infantry or personal two-handed builds. |
| Polearm | The first two tiers are mostly damage/cavalry-control tools. Good for polearm users, less general than Trade or Medicine. |
| Bow | Great for personal archers or archer captains: movement accuracy, headshot damage, armor penetration, reload movement. |
| Crossbow | Strong if you personally use crossbows or captain crossbow troops; otherwise it is not a general party splash. |
| Throwing | Good for throwing builds and formations; `Flexible Fighter` is especially interesting because it grants formation skill bonuses across Control/Vigor depending on troop type. |

## Practical Rule

For a broad campaign build, the first splash candidates are usually:

1. Trade to 50 for profit and price marking.
2. Medicine to 25 or 50 for post-battle recovery.
3. Steward, Scouting, or Leadership to 50 if the main hero is filling that party role.
4. Athletics or Riding to 50 if the character personally fights on foot or mounted.
5. Weapon skills only when they match the actual combat plan.
